This is the year 2017


All public Bluebike data began in January 2015.

There were a total of 1,313,774 total bike rides recorded during this year.

An increase of 6.27% compared to the previous year, and 17.03% increase since 2015.


Based on this chart we are able to see the rides for the year 2017 broken down by month.
Clearly, the summer and warm months have a larger amount of rides then in the winter.
February recorded the least rides (17.27k) and August is recorded having the most rides (190.65k).
Therefore, we can see an increase of 1,003.94% from February to August in total rides per month.
For the second year in a row the proportion of increase from the least to most popular months has decreased. This can actually be seen as beneficial, as it concludes that the amounth of monthly rides are becoming more consist on a month to month basis, and not having only a few months that spike in rides.

Bluebike categories users by two types, Customer and Subscriber.
A Subscriber has a monthly or annual membership while a Customer is a single use ride.
Continuously the Subscriber proportion has increased since 2015 (by 27.42%), which gives Bluebike a more stable customer basis than that of Customer single use riders

When looking at the average trip during (in seconds) by user type, we notice for the first time a more consistant trend without outliers.
This limited variation in average ride time by user type on a month to month basis can be reflected due to a more regular customer basis.
The average trip duration for Customers is 3,160 seconds (or 52.66 minutes). and the average trip duration for Subscribers is 763.28 seconds (or 12.72 minutes).
It is notable however though, that Customers have average rides that are 313.99% longer than that of Subscribers.

The top 5 annually most started from Bluebike stations are indicated in this graph, along with their corresponding Subscriber and User amounts.
The most popular station is at MIT with 35,750 total rides in 2017. For the second year in a row this station has been the most started from Bluebike station.
Subscibers with memberships account for 89.40% of all rides at the top 5 most used starting stations, a larger proportion than the previous year by 8.53%.

The top 5 annually most started from Bluebike stations are indicated in this graph, along with their corresponding Subscriber and User amounts.
The most popular station is at MIT with 32,150 total rides in 2017. Notably again, this station remains as the most used, making it important for Bluebike.
Consistently, we still see the majority of riders ending at the top 5 finishing stations are Subscribers, accounting for 89.76% of the rides.

The median amount of rides that finish at an ending station is 2,420 with first and third quartiles having 74 and 7,540 rides respectively. We can note that the trend continues to fall of first quartile values for the average amount of rides, concluding that more stations have become less used.
This year, we can see the most amount of stations being considered outliers, 10 in total, showing they are significantly used more frequently.
Considering this large disparity, having these most used stations be maintained properly is vital to the success of Bluebike due to their popularity.

Gender is indicated in this graph by 1 = Male, 2 = Female, 0 = No response.
As we can see, this is the first year where all the Customers did not report gender.
Subscribers continue to be male dominated, being 75.53% of their riders. Female subscribers making up 24.29% of riders and non-responding subscribers account for 0.61%.
Based on these demographics continuing to be a consistant trend, it would be advisable for Bluebike to promote to their female audience.

Users of Bluebike have the ability to report their birth year. For 210,947 of the rides (16.06%) birth year was not reported.
The most amount of rides were found to have been taken by respondents born in 1989, making them 28 in 2017.
The millennial generation (born between 1981 and 1999) represents the largest proportion of Bluebike riders (89.93%).
