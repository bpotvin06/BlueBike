This is the year 2016


All public Bluebike data began in January 2015.

There were a total of 1,236,203 total bike rides recorded during this year.

An increase of 10.12% compared to the previous year.


Based on this chart we are able to see the rides for the year 2016 broken down by month.
Clearly, the summer and warm months have a larger amount of rides then in the winter.
January recorded the least rides (12.06k) and August is recorded having the most rides (174.2k).
Therefore, we can see an increase of 1,344.4% from January to August in total rides per month.
However, this proportion of increase is less than that noted the previous year, which had an increase of 4,261% from its least to most popular months to take Bluebike rides.

Bluebike categories users by two types, Customer and Subscriber.
A Subscriber has a monthly or annual membership while a Customer is a single use ride.
We notice a change in user types since 2015, as Subscribers increased by 20.67% and Customers decreased by 38.18%.

When looking at the average trip during (in seconds) by user type, we notice a significant outlier in Customers during December.
These outlying customers had an average trip of 23,520 seconds (or 6.53 hours) in December 2016.
The winter of 2016 marked the first time in Bluebike history that all stations remained active and not closing due to weather.

The top 5 annually most started from Bluebike stations are indicated in this graph, along with their corresponding Subscriber and User amounts.
The most popular station is at MIT with 35,650 total rides in 2016. This can larger be due to this location being on the Cambridge side of the Harvard Bridge, which connects Cambridge to Boston.
Subscibers with memberships account for 80.87% of all rides at the top 5 most used starting stations, a larger proportion than the previous year.

The top 5 annually most started from Bluebike stations are indicated in this graph, along with their corresponding Subscriber and User amounts.
The most popular station is at MIT with 36,110 total rides in 2016. For the first time we can note the top starting and endind stations are the same, with only a 460 ride difference between them.
Consistently, we still see the majority of riders ending at the top 5 finishing stations are Subscribers, accounting for 80.87% of the rides.

The median amount of rides that finish at an ending station is 1,930 with first and third quartiles having 232 and 7,820 rides respectively. This is a significant difference and decrease when compared to the results from 2015.
Therefore, we can see the top 5 end stations are all exceeding triple the amount of end rides then the third quartile value with all 5 top ending stations even being true outliers.
Considering this large disparity, having the most used stations be maintained properly is vital to the success of Bluebike.

Gender is indicated in this graph by 1 = Male, 2 = Female, 0 = No response.
Males account for 61.09% of total rides in 2016 (increase of 5.55%), with females representing 20.12% (decrease of 1.23%), and non-responding rides were 18.79% (decrease of 13.45%).
When looking by user type the Subscribers have a much greater proportion of males, making up 73.12% of their total rides. Females accounted for 24.19% of rides and non-responding customers were only 2.69%.
For the second year in a row, Bluebikes have been reportly used by males at a much greater proportion.

Users of Bluebike have the ability to report their birth year. For 226,610 of the rides (18.33%) birth year was not reported.
Again the most amount of rides were found to have been taken by respondents born in 1988, making them 28 in 2016.
